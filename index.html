Ministry

What is the Gospel?

Gospel is just a word like any other — it specifically means good news. But what good news? Costco and Walmart have good news. So what good news are we talking about?

The gospel is the good news that you can know you're going to heaven when you die — before you die.

"These things have I written unto you that believe on the name of the Son of God; that ye may know that ye have eternal life."

1 John 5:13 · KJV

Point 1

All Have Sinned

Everyone is a sinner, less perfect than God. The Virgin Mary, the Pope, your minister, your mother, you and I — all are sinners.

"For all have sinned and come short of the glory of God."

Romans 3:23 · KJV

"For there is not a just man upon earth, that doeth good and sinneth not."

Ecclesiastes 7:20 · KJV
✦ More scripture to be added — Is. 64:6, 1 John 3:4, Jam. 4:17, Jam. 2:10, Rom. 7:21

Point 2

Eternal Separation

The result and penalty of sin is death — separation from God. Sin is not paid for by good works, penance, church membership, water baptism, etc. Sin is paid for by death. God does not hate the sinner, but He hates the sin. He hates sin because it separates us from Him, and He doesn't want us to be separated from Him.

"For the wages of sin is death…"

Romans 6:23 · KJV

"In flaming fire taking vengeance on them that know not God and that obey not the gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ: Who shall be punished with everlasting destruction from the presence of the Lord and from the glory of His power."

2 Thessalonians 1:8–9 · KJV
✦ More scripture to be added — Rev. 20:15, Luke 16:19-31, Nah. 1:3a, Isa. 59:2

Point 3

Absolute Perfection

The eternal heaven will be a perfect place. No sin could enter this heaven. Man must be perfectly righteous to go to heaven.

"Nevertheless we, according to His promise, look for new heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth righteousness."

2 Peter 3:13 · KJV
✦ More scripture to be added — Matt. 5:48, Rom. 3:23

Point 4

Not of Works

Nothing man could do could help him obtain the absolute perfection God requires for eternal life.

"For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: It is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast."

Ephesians 2:8–9 · KJV
✦ More scripture to be added — Tit. 3:5, Rom. 4:5, Rom. 11:6

Point 5

Sufficient Payment

Christ made a complete payment for all sin and offers His righteousness. Christ never sinned. He lived a perfect life. Yet He took our sins upon Himself and paid for them. He did this so He could give us God's righteousness in place of our sin.

"For He hath made Him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in Him."

2 Corinthians 5:21 · KJV
✦ More scripture to be added — Heb. 9:22, Heb. 9:14, Lev. 17:11

Point 6

Believe and Receive

All man must do to be saved is to BELIEVE Jesus was the Lord who paid for all his sin — RECEIVE Christ as his Saviour. God gives eternal life only to those who will accept it by faith alone.

✦ More scripture to be added — John 6:47, John 3:16, John 5:24, Luke 8:12, John 6:28-29

Point 7

Everlasting Means Forever

Man can know he has eternal life now because eternal life IS eternal. Notice — the gift of God is ETERNAL LIFE. Not "life until you sin again," nor "life until you backslide."

"For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord."

Romans 6:23 · KJV
✦ More scripture to be added — 2 Tim. 2:13, 2 Pet. 3:9, John 6:37

Ministry

Family Mission Statement

This is not doctrine — it is the frame our household uses to guide our decisions. Anyone who believes Jesus died for their sins is my brother or sister in Christ. Feel free to steal it or make up one for your family.

We believe Jesus, who is called the Christ, died for the sin and iniquity of the entire world. And because we believe his sacrifice was for us, He will take us to heaven to be with God for eternity.

Because we believe this, we find it morally and eternally imperative to tell other people this good news, so that they can put their faith in Christ and go to heaven as well.

Due to this imperative our mission as a household is to be found faithful with whatsoever we have and use it for the furtherance of the gospel being shared with others.

Therefore, our choices are the foundation of our faithfulness based on the Word of God.

Let our words be pleasing unto the Lord. Let our actions serve his purposes and not our own. Let us rely solely on Jesus and His Word.

Street Preaching

Are you going to heaven?
You can know.
HWY 78 & 56
Bonham, TX

I stand on the corner of Highway 78 and 56 in Bonham, Texas and hold a bunch of signs. The chief sign says: Are you going to heaven? You can know.

I had the idea during a Bible study when we went through 1 Corinthians chapter 1. When we got to verse 21 I just laughed out loud, because I had this image in my head of me getting laughed at holding up a sign in my truck bed. Everyone just looked at me for my strange unexpected outburst, and I explained myself — to which my best friend said 'well now you have to do it.' And so I did, and now I do.

Don't know how long I will do it for or how profitable it is on this side of heaven. But to the people who stopped by and changed their minds and put their faith in Christ: worth it.

Jailhouse Ministry

I asked the Lord for an opportunity to serve, and He put it in my way. He gave me the choice of whether or not to take it, and I had the desire; so I went for it. I preach one sermon to a different group of guys each week.

The sermon works through 1 Corinthians chapter 3 — establishing the gospel first, then showing from Paul's letter to the Corinthians why God leaves believers on the earth after salvation, what good works are and are not, and how God will reward each believer's choices at the judgment seat of Christ.

I'm not allowed to bring in anything to the jail but a Bible — no cameras or other materials. But I want people to feel involved in this ministry. On my YouTube channel I'm working on putting up a 'Q&A from the Jail' so that others can see how good the inmates' questions are, and the responses I gave from the Bible — with a follow up answer if I didn't have a biblical answer at the time and needed to go and study.

Inventions

The intent of building inventions is as a hook for the gospel. I have a utility patent for a prototype and am working on my next prototype currently.

In Genesis 1:28 God gives clear instructions to mankind to 'subdue the earth' — meaning to take raw materials and build something from them. By designing and building prototypes I hope to show others Christ through the work of my hands. All for the honor and glory of the Lord. Matthew 5:16.

Check out some of my work on Github that I "vibecoded" as the youths call it.

Queries are handled by Charles, an AI phone receptionist agent, who takes down your information and notifies me later — so as not to interrupt work or ministry.

Log Disposal
